Meta Campaigns · Bull Boulder Tow Hitches

How I Flipped a Meta Campaign Upside Down

2 out of 6 videos became winners in an industry where the owner told me 1 in 15 is the typical ratio. Here's exactly how, and what the numbers say.

Meta Ads Video Creative DTC ROAS Active Campaign
Apr 2026

The Setup

Bull Boulder Tow Hitches is a direct-to-consumer brand in an industry I'd never worked in before. Before launching a single dollar of paid spend, I did the homework, cross-referencing competitor ads, studying what their buyers actually respond to, and building hooks that felt native to the audience rather than like ads.

The brief from Nate H., founder and owner:

"In this industry, if you get 1 winner out of every 15 videos, you're doing well."

We launched 6 videos. 2 became clear winners. That's a 2-in-6 hit rate, more than 4x the industry benchmark, in a category I'd never touched before.

The Creative Strategy

Each video was built around a different hook angle, problem-aware, aspirational, and entertainment-led. The goal wasn't to guess; it was to generate enough creative variants that the data could tell us what the market actually wanted.

  • Researched competitor creative on Meta Ad Library before writing a single script
  • Wrote CTAs that felt brand-appropriate, not generic drop-ship language
  • Built hooks specifically for the audience that shops for tow hitches, practical buyers, not impulse
  • Tested across Video Ads Testing Broad (6 active ads, $1,200/day budget)

The Numbers, Last 30 Days

The report covers the three videos from the testing set. Two are still running. One was turned off after the data revealed a structural problem with audience intent.

Last 30 days · Meta Ads · Video Ads Testing Broad · $1,200/day budget. Flash Sale 1 | Video 4 is the benchmark performer, highest ROAS (2.65×), lowest CPA ($99.82), and strongest CVR at 1.68%. Holy **** | Video 6 leads on impressions and hook rate (52.45%) but CVR has room to grow, signaling a strong hook with a possible offer or landing page gap.

Breaking Down the Winners

Flash Sale 1 | Video 4, The best performer across every metric that matters. 2.65× ROAS, $99.82 CPA, 1.68% CVR. Nate flagged something important here:

"Flash sale prints hard despite the low hook rate because Meta is using it as a bottom of funnel ad, or a potential one shot." Attributed to Nate H.

Translation: Meta's algorithm identified this creative as a closer, not a prospector. It's converting the people who already know they need a tow hitch. That's a signal to protect this spend and let the algorithm work.

Holy **** | Video 6, The volume driver. 945,356 impressions, 52.45% hook rate (top performer in the set), $1.30 CPC. CVR at 0.84% has room to improve, which points to a post-click issue, likely the offer or landing page, rather than the creative itself failing.

"Yeah, they're peaking now and then will start to decline. Their budget changes through the 30 days." Attributed to Nate H.

What We Turned Off & Why

Hook Up Gone Wrong | Video 3 had a 44.52% hook rate and the cheapest CPC in the set at $0.63, both strong signals. But CVR sat at 0.25% and ROAS never crossed 1.02×. The creative attracted clicks, not customers. High CTR, wrong audience intent. We turned it off, kept the learnings on what hooks attract curiosity vs. buying intent, and folded that into the next round of scripts.

Full account view, Video Ads Testing Broad, 6 active ads. The two $1,200/day budget lines (Flash Sale 1 | Video 4 and Holy **** | Video 6) are the signals we scale from. Everything else informs the next creative sprint.

The Takeaway

New industry. No playbook. A 2-in-6 creative hit rate against a 1-in-15 industry norm. The process that made it work wasn't luck, it was research before spend, hooks built for the actual buyer, and a testing structure that let the best creative rise without burning budget on guesses.

This campaign is still live and scaling. More creative rounds coming.

Meta Campaign & Geo-Targeting · Pavers and Turf by O'Neill's

From Zero to 59 Leads a Week, at $5 Each

A 17-week build for a local landscaper. Flat budget, creative refreshed weekly. Volume climbed, cost per lead fell, the inverse did the work.

Meta Ads Lead Gen Geo-Targeting Local Services
Mar 2026

The Setup

Pavers and Turf by O'Neill's is a local landscape company. No brand recognition beyond word-of-mouth, no paid acquisition history. The brief: can Meta carry lead flow for a small regional contractor the same way it carries DTC brands?

The Approach

Flat $50/day budget from week one. Creative refreshed on a rolling cadence, winners scaled, losers paused, new angles fed in. Every ad built around a single job-site moment: the install, the reveal, the finished product.

  • TikTok-style cuts pulled from live install footage, not staged
  • Geo-targeted to zip codes within realistic drive-time
  • Lead form on-platform, no landing page friction
  • Creative rotated weekly to keep fatigue down and the algorithm curious

The Result

CPA dropped from $26 to $5, an 81% reduction on the same budget. One reel alone drove 193 leads at $5.25 CPL. By week 17 the account was sitting at 59 leads/week, up from 12 at launch.

Athlete Collaboration · IM8 × Jewell Loyd

IM8 × Jewell Loyd: Athlete Content, Done Clean

Collab content for a 1.3M-audience athlete with IM8. Entertaining, brand-safe, and built to fit her page, not interrupt it.

Brand Athlete Native Content Cross-Platform
Feb 2026

The Brief

A single collaboration post with Jewell Loyd (WNBA, Team USA) for IM8. The brand needed presence on a top-tier athlete feed without forcing a scripted ad moment. The test: can a partner placement feel native enough that the 1.3M audience doesn't skip?

The Approach

Lead with the athlete, not the product. IM8 integrates as part of her routine, not as the subject of the frame. One clean shoot day, minimal scripting, intentional pacing that matched the rhythm of Jewell's existing feed.

  • Pre-shoot scroll of her feed to match tone, color, and cadence
  • Product placement as a background beat, not the centerpiece
  • Final edit cleared by the athlete team same-day, no back-and-forth
  • Delivered in both feed and reel cuts for cross-platform flexibility

The Result

Post landed natively, no "sponsored" fatigue in the comments, no drop in engagement vs. her organic posts. Brand got the athlete co-sign without paying the credibility tax that usually comes with big placements.

Content Design · YouTube · ~70K subs

Clickbait Thumbnails for a Sports Creator

Simple hooks, bright reactions, same-day turnarounds. Built for how people actually scroll YouTube, obvious, bold, hard to ignore.

Content Design YouTube Thumbnails Sports
Jan 2026

The Brief

Thumbnails for a sports creator covering NFL slates, Super Bowl picks, and live streams. The creator needed thumbnails that stop the YouTube scroll and earn the click on every single upload, same-day turnaround, no misses.

The Approach

Every thumbnail designed around one thing: one clear reaction, one clear hook. No clutter, no copy-paste templates. Each live slate gets fresh art, because the scroll doesn't reward "good enough".

  • 1280×720, tailored not templated, every frame built for the specific upload
  • Same-day turnaround on live slates (NBA/NFL props, game-day reactions)
  • 2-4 words max hook on every frame, the scroll doesn't read
  • High-contrast color, bright reactions, big-moment energy
