How it played out

Launch. Iterate. Unlock.

Weeks 1–5 · Launch
$26 → $15
CPA, cold start

Opened with a spread of static and short-form. Week one ran hot at $26 a lead while the algo was still learning the geo. By week 5 it had nearly halved.

Weeks 6–10 · Iterate
$13–$19
CPA, refresh #1

First creative refresh. Paused the losers, dropped in new hooks, tightened the audience. CPA stabilized in the mid-teens while the lead count crept from 17 to 22 a week.

Weeks 11–17 · Unlock
$8 → $5
CPA, refresh #2

Second refresh introduced the Bocce reel — a TikTok-style transition edit shot on a live install. The algo found its pocket. Leads jumped from 22 to 59 a week, CPA collapsed to $5.

What the winners looked like

One creative did the heavy lifting.

The Bocce reel pulled 40% of total leads at $5.25 each — a TikTok-style quick-cut transition edit of an existing install, no extra production. The pattern is clear: more videos in that register, fewer static images.

Google search runs $8–$30 a click in the paver / turf category. By week 17 Meta at geo-level is running $0.08 a click on this account. Same buyer, same service radius — a cheaper way in, and it scales with the installs already being photographed on-site.

The ad that ran away with it.

The Bocce reel — TikTok-style transitions cut from a live install. 193 leads, $5.25 CPL, $1,012 total spend.

The playbook: refresh every 5 weeks. Let the algo do the rest. Week 1's $26 CPA wasn't a failure — it was the entry price. Two creative refreshes later we're at $5, and the budget never moved. The creative did the work.
