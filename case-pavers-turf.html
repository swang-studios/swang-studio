Meta Campaign · Geo-Targeted · Local Contractor

A Meta Campaign Anyone Can Start.

Local contractor advertising on Meta is a wide-open lane. Most of the spend in the category piles into Google search at $8 to $30 a click, and the social side sits almost untouched, cheap, geo-precise, and starving for actual content. Pavers and Turf by O'Neill's is the proof.

The Loop

Projects become content. Content becomes ads. Ads bring the next project back.

It's an inverse-built funnel: the work is the inventory and the ad spend is the multiplier. Hover any step.

Why this works. The home-services category on Meta is one of the last places in paid social where geo-targeted ad inventory is genuinely cheap. You're not bidding against direct-to-consumer brands burning $40 CPMs, you're bidding against a handful of other local contractors, most of whom aren't even running creative.

The flywheel is inverse-built: instead of writing copy to fit an ad, the install is the ad. Every job site becomes the next campaign's raw material. The cost of the content collapses because it's already happening, you're just showing up to capture it.

Meta vs Google · Local Contractor Lane

Same buyer. Wildly different cost to reach them.

Google Search Ads
$8 to $30
per click
  • Bid auction packed with national franchises and lead-aggregator middlemen
  • Intent is high, but the cost to enter is brutal for a local operator
  • You pay for words, not the work, no creative leverage
Meta (Pavers and Turf)
$1 to $3
per qualified click, geo-targeted
  • Almost no local competition running real creative in the same radius
  • Each install becomes the ad, content cost effectively rolls into the job
  • Lookalikes compound off real customers, not keywords

Numbers are directional, based on observed local-contractor benchmarks. The point isn't the exact figure, it's the order of magnitude. You can run a serious Meta test on what gets eaten by a single Google week.
