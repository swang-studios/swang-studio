From zero to 59 leads a week. At $5 each.

Same budget. 5× more leads coming in. Each one costing 81% less than before. Here's how we got the line to bend.

Pavers and Turf by O'Neill's · Meta 17 weeks · ongoing

The starting point

When we picked up the account, every new lead was costing around twenty-six dollars. Twelve leads a week on a good week. The budget wasn't the issue — the creative was. Ads were tired, targeting was broad, and the best-performing reel hadn't been touched in months.

Before

$26

→

After 17 weeks

$5

What changed

Instead of running a big-bang rebuild, we moved in short cycles. Every two to three weeks I'd bring in a fresh batch of ads, watch the metrics for a week, keep whatever was pulling weight, and quietly retire the rest.

Winners compound. Losers get replaced. Budget never moves — the creative does the work.

By week eight, a single reel — TikTok-style transitions cut from live installs — was pulling in leads at $5.25 each. That one ad did the heaviest lifting; nearly half the total volume over the run.

Leads that convert

Volume is nice. Deals are better. Of the 475 leads that came through the door, 5 to 10 percent converted into signed projects. Each project starts at a $10,000 floor — that's the minimum deal size for this market.
